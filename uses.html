Using  The Retro Algorithm

Part1. Approved Usage

Retro watch-next algorithms are subject to a degree of unpredictabity and often inappropriateness in their suggested watch-next movie results. Owing to this side-effect, a number of suggested healthy and governmentally-approved uses have been both proposed and capitalised upon over the decades for the enjoyment of patrons. These may be summarised as follows...

Viewer Stagnation is a well researched theory that you may study out on your own time. The principal tenet of which is that after a period of time, viewers will lapse into a pattern of watching the same genre, style, or storylines.

It ought to make sense then that the opportunity for randomness in the results from the retro watch-next algorithm may be capitalised upon here. If you feel you are suffering from Viewer Stagnation, please consult your medical practitioner at your earliest opportunity. However, if you have been vaccinated already, you may use the recreated algorithm here at "You May Also Like" to suppress any residual symptoms of VS. Just enter your watch-history and begin.

For any given watch-history that you may provide to the algorithm, there is a corresponding set of watch-next movie suggestions returned. One popular game from the 1970s involved using the retro algorithm in reverse.

How? By thinking of a certain movie. Then entering your watch-history. But doing so in such a way that the movie you thought of is returned as a watch-next suggestion. Be careful though! The algorithm can be unpredictable. So think more randomly, and less predictably for fun times and success!

In the 1990s science had yet to discover computers or binge-watching. In a society lacking the knowledge given to us by American Pie and the Saw franchise, people turned instead to alternative movie Saturdays.

A joyful single pizza slice and light beer were the traditionally suggested accompaniments to alt-movie night. Chosen in order not to spoil the entertainment or engender rowdiness. Should you engage in using the algorithm to source alt-movies for your alt-movie Saturday, you may instead choose a half-glass of wine. Or stick with water.

Part2. Perilous Misuse and Miscreancy

Of course, there are always those that seek to rail against what is naturally wholesome. If you are one of those folk, it would be remiss of "You May Also Like", churlish at worst not to mention those activities. Activities, it must be repeated, that are governmentally-unsanctioned. Such as these stem from the frequently inaccurate results yielded from retro watch-next algorithms. These must necessarily come with a warning. It is well documented that participants in such activities have been overcome by movie madness and various other physical perturbances as a direct result. Caveat Emptor!

Pushing the envelope of the algorithm by seeding it not with regular movie titles and accepting the unpredictability of the results. Instead, patrons seeded the retro algorithm's watch history with edge-case movies, many called the activity the Strangest Of The Strange.

It was widely reported in the late 1980s what participants were widely afflicted with uncontrollable laughter and rampant optimism, neither of which ought to be taken lightly as a potential hazard should you choose to dabble with algorithm misuse in such a fashion.

A topic for the strong of constitution though it was reported that some users of the retro algorithm deployed it to ends that were altogether unwholesome. Such users were apparently motivated to inflict movie distress upon themselves.

Without too much elaboration This act involved the user subjecting themselves (voluntarily, it might be added) to watching whichever movies the algorithm returned regardless of their own health, safety or sanity. And not just singly, but consuming all of the algorithm's movies in one sitting. Or perhaps lying. It's unclear from the autopsies. If you must engage in such behaviour, do so at your own peril!
